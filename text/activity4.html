The overall uncertainty in an analysis, \(e_{analysis}\) is a function of the standard deviation for sampling, \(s_{samp}\), the standard deviation for the analytical method, \(s_{meth}\), the number of samples, \(n_{samp}\), the number of replicate analyses per sample, \(n_{rep}\), and the confidence interval.

\[e_{analysis} = t \times \sqrt{\frac{s_{samp}^2}{n_{samp}} + \frac{s_{meth}^2}{n_{samp} \times n_{rep}}}\]
